## 1. Brand Foundation

### Purpose

Layer8Culture Radio is a focus music and visual atmosphere brand for builders, creators, coders, students, entrepreneurs, designers, and dreamers.

It blends **Afrofuturist lo-fi**, coding/study beats, global rhythms, and cinematic animated environments into long-form sessions designed for deep work, creative focus, and disciplined execution.

Layer8Culture Radio is not just background music. It is a branded world for people who are building something.

### Positioning

**Layer8Culture Radio is the premium lo-fi coding radio station for culturally grounded builders.**

It sits at the intersection of:

- Technology
- Culture
- Music
- Deep work
- AI-assisted creativity
- Afrofuturist imagination
- Builder discipline

### Brand Promise

When someone presses play on Layer8Culture Radio, they should feel:

- Focused
- Inspired
- Calm
- Productive
- Culturally seen
- Creatively energized
- Ready to build

### Brand Personality

Layer8Culture Radio should feel:

- Premium

## 8. Motion & Animation Guidelines

Layer8Culture Radio animation should be subtle, loop-friendly, and calming.

### Best Elements to Animate

Use these repeatedly across videos:

- Slow code scrolling
- Blinking cursor
- Soft graph pulse
- Gentle coffee steam
- Neon sign glow
- Desk lamp breathing glow
- Plant sway
- Hanging vine movement
- City lights twinkling
- Distant flying vehicles
- Morning/sunset haze
- Dust particles in light
- Minimal shoulder breathing
- Slight hoodie fabric movement

### Motion Rules

Use:

- Locked camera
- Minimal push-in only for intros
- Slow ambient movement
- Smooth loops
- No dramatic scene changes
- 6–12 second loops

Avoid:

- Fast camera movement
- Character turning around
- Face reveal
- Large hand movement
- Aggressive flicker
- Warped text
- Object morphing
- Chaotic vehicle motion
- Too much cloud movement

### Loop Duration

Recommended:

- **6 seconds** for testing
- **8 seconds** for final loops
- **10–12 seconds** only if the model stays stable

Best default:

**8 seconds, 16:9, 1080p, subtle motion, locked camera.**

## 9. Music Direction

### Core Sound

Layer8Culture Radio music should be instrumental, lo-fi, chillhop-adjacent, warm, focus-friendly, loopable, soulful, non-distracting, culturally inspired, premium, and polished.

### Sonic Palette

Use:

- Rhodes piano
- Soft electric piano
- Muted drums
- Dusty boom-bap drums
- Smooth bass
- Ambient synth pads
- Vinyl texture
- Tape warmth
- Subtle African percussion
- Light Afrobeat influence
- Reggae bass for reggae sessions
- Guitar textures for Latin sessions
- Upright bass and brushed drums for jazz sessions

Avoid:

- Strong vocals
- Big drops
- Aggressive trap patterns unless specifically used for a “build mode” session
- Overly busy melodies
- Harsh synths
- Distracting solos
- Loud risers
- Overly cinematic orchestral builds

### BPM Guidelines

| Theme | BPM Range | Energy |
|---|---:|---|
| Deep Night Focus | 70–82 | Calm, immersive |
| Morning Build | 80–86 | Optimistic, steady |
| Sunset Flow | 76–84 | Reflective, warm |
| Rainy Night | 68–78 | Cozy, slow |
| Jazz Coding Lounge | 72–84 | Smooth, sophisticated |
| Reggae Focus | 78–88 | Warm, relaxed groove |
| Latin Study Beats | 80–92 | Warm, rhythmic, light |
| Hip-Hop Build Beats | 82–94 | Head-nod, productive |
| Trap Build Mode | 88–110 | More energetic, still focused |

### Three-Part Music Structure

Each long video should include:

1. **Beginning Track** — welcomes the listener into the world
2. **Middle Track** — supports deep work and carries the session
3. **Ending Track** — winds down smoothly and reflects completion

## 19. Do / Don’t Summary

### Do

- Keep everything cinematic and premium
- Use strong visual consistency
- Keep animation subtle
- Build a recognizable universe
- Use the same character/studio language
- Keep titles searchable
- Use playlists intentionally
- Create social posts for every video
- Use the 8 radio icon consistently
- Document music licensing and prompts

### Don’t

- Overload visuals with text
- Use copyrighted references
- Use chaotic motion
- Create random unrelated themes
- Make every video look totally different
- Use distracting vocals
- Overuse AI process language in every post
- Make the brand feel like a generic lo-fi channel
